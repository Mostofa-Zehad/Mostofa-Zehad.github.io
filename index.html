Hi! I’m Kazi Zehad Mostofa

Graduate Research Assistant | Forecasting | Microgrid Optimization | Energy Sustainability

I’m a Ph.D. researcher at Clarkson University focused on building energy systems that work beyond the lab. My work spans deterministic and probabilistic load forecasting, renewable integration, and microgrid control, backed by hands-on data engineering in Python. I’ve led projects on smart buildings, hybrid energy systems, and scenario-based microgrid optimisation—linking forecasting, Gaussian-copula scenario generation, and Pyomo-based dispatch to real operating constraints. My recent project collaborated with the U.S. Department of Energy and GE Vernova was a microgrid optimization and economic dispatch for Tok School, Alaska to improve reliability, cut costs, and reduce emissions for a remote community. My current project is about energy market analysis in colab with NYISO.

To explore my recent work, I would like to welcome you to visit my profiles in Google Scholar & LinkedIn.
