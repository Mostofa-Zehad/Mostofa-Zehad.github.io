All-PV Blended System for Energy-Efficient Net-Zero Buildings

This project presents an advanced solar energy system that integrates rooftop PV, solar windows, and Building Integrated Photovoltaics (BIPV), combined with AI-enabled performance optimization and real-time IoT monitoring. The objective was to achieve high energy self-sufficiency and smart energy control across diverse urban settings.

Project Highlights

  • Achieved up to 78% energy self-sufficiency in simulated buildings.
  • Deployed ML models to optimize PV component configuration and energy dispatch.
  • Compared energy yield across multiple global cities with varying weather and sunlight profiles.
  • Enabled real-time remote performance analytics and fault detection using IoT and ThingSpeak.

System Architecture

AI algorithms were trained on environmental and solar datasets to forecast energy generation, adjust loads dynamically, and recommend system configurations for peak efficiency.

IoT Monitoring & Cloud Dashboard

ThingSpeak was used as the real-time dashboard interface to visualize live data including solar irradiance, system voltage/current, temperature, and efficiency metrics. Alert mechanisms were included for anomaly detection and system diagnostics.

Simulation & Performance Comparison

The blended system was tested across cities like Tokyo, Kuala Lumpur, London, and New York. Simulations showed high consistency in energy gains with minor location-specific tuning, making the system globally deployable.

Energy Management Layout

The layout demonstrates interactions between PV units, inverters, batteries, smart controllers, and building loads. The system also includes predictive fault isolation and recovery features.

Conclusion

This project highlights a scalable, AI-enhanced, and IoT-enabled photovoltaic architecture suitable for modern urban sustainability goals. It contributes toward practical deployment of net-zero buildings and bridges the gap between smart monitoring and energy-efficient hardware.
