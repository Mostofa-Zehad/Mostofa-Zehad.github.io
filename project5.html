71.4 MW Wind Farm Conceptual Design in Binh Thuan, Vietnam

Project Summary

This project outlines a comprehensive plan to harness wind energy in Vietnam’s Binh Thuan Province using Vestas V150-4.2 MW turbines. The 71.4 MW onshore wind farm optimizes site conditions, economic feasibility, and energy yield, benchmarked globally for competitiveness and efficiency.

1. Introduction & Motivation

Vietnam targets 30% renewable energy by 2030. Binh Thuan, with 7.45 m/s wind speeds and flat topography, presents ideal conditions for utility-scale onshore wind development.

2. Site Selection & Comparative Analysis

Sites in Connecticut (CT), Texas (TX), and Vietnam (VN) were compared. Vietnam offered superior wind density, low regulatory friction, and cost effectiveness.

3. Turbine Selection & Layout

The wind farm uses 17 Vestas V150 turbines in a 6-6-5 layout, based on wind rose data. The configuration minimizes wake effects and maximizes output.

4. Infrastructure & Monitoring

The site is adjacent to a 110 kV transmission line. Two met towers will monitor wind flow over the 12 km² site, supporting long-term energy forecasting and validation.

5. Economic Analysis

  • Total CAPEX: $127.7 million (~$1,788/kW)
  • NCF: 41.6% → AEP: 260,550 MWh/year
  • LCOE: $65–70/MWh
  • Key Costs: Turbines (52%), Balance of Plant (17%), Towers (12%)

6. Risk Management Framework

  • Wind Variability: Addressed by met towers and CFD models
  • Construction Risk: Mitigated by geotech surveys + experienced EPCs
  • Regulatory Risk: IFC-aligned stakeholder engagement
  • Wake Loss: Optimized turbine spacing and yaw control
  • Cost Volatility: Managed by fixed-price contracts and financial buffers
  • Environmental/Social: ESIA compliance and community dialogue

7. Conclusion & Path Forward

This conceptual design confirms the strong technical and economic viability of wind power in Binh Thuan. It sets a benchmark for replicable models in Southeast Asia—delivering clean, affordable energy while strengthening grid resilience.

  • 17 turbines, 71.4 MW total capacity
  • AEP: 260,550 MWh/year | NCF: 41.6%
  • LCOE: $65–70/MWh | CAPEX: $127.7 million
