Design and Implementation of a Cost-effective Cold Storage Unit Powered by Hybrid Energy Sources

1. Introduction

In Bangladesh, the lack of cold storage facilities in remote agricultural areas contributes heavily to food spoilage and economic loss. This project aims to address this issue by designing a hybrid energy-powered cold storage unit that combines solar, wind, battery, and grid resources to deliver uninterrupted refrigeration with minimized environmental impact and maximum cost-efficiency.

2. Methodology and Load Profile

We analyzed an 800-tonne fish storage (temperature: -18°C to -24°C) and a 40-tonne crop storage (0°C to 4°C), consuming around 459,637 kWh/year. Load profiles were mapped and multiple hybrid configurations simulated using HOMER Pro. The best model minimizes excess energy while achieving ROI within 5 years.

  • Solar PV: 500 kW array
  • Wind Turbine: 1 unit (1.5 kW)
  • Grid Connection: Buy/sell rate set to $0.10/$0.05 per kWh
  • Converter: 372 kW
  • Battery: Optional for smoothing peaks

3. Power Output & Performance

The system produces 1,180,219 kWh/year, with a minimal 1.78% of energy left unused. Solar contributes 61.4% of total output. Wind supplements during non-daylight hours. Grid provides backup only when required, making the system 64% renewable on average.

4. Economic Analysis

The total system cost is ~$341,000. Major costs include PV panels ($70,579), wind turbines ($33,175), converters ($74,817), and grid O&M ($162,496). Payback period is ~4.4 years with an IRR of 22%. Carbon savings exceed 13,000 kg COâ‚‚ annually.

5. System Optimization and Automation

  • Arduino-based smart switching
  • Real-time monitoring to optimize compressor use
  • Thin-film coating reduces cooling demand by 120 kWh/day
  • Smart scheduling minimizes peak hour operation

6. Conclusion

This hybrid system achieves grid independence up to 75%, rapid ROI, and a scalable model for rural cold chains. By integrating automation and material science, it bridges energy access and food security in developing regions. Further optimization can be achieved through smart metering and IoT-enhanced control systems.
