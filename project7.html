❄️ Sustainable Cold Storage Facility Powered by Hybrid Energy Sources

Introduction

In Bangladesh, rural post-harvest losses are rampant due to the absence of cold storage. This project presents a hybrid-powered cold storage design that tackles this issue by combining solar, wind, and grid electricity to reduce operational cost and environmental impact.

Methodology & Load Profile

Based on 800-tonne fish and 40-tonne crop requirements, the total load demand was ~459,637 kWh/year. The solution was optimized using HOMER Pro.

  • 500 kW Solar PV
  • 1.5 kW Wind Turbine
  • Grid Sell/Buy: $0.05/$0.10
  • 372 kW Converter

HOMER Pro Optimal Configuration

After simulating 20+ scenarios, HOMER identified a PV + wind + grid model as the most cost-effective, with only 1.78% excess electricity.

Power Output & Comparative Performance

  • All-PV: High surplus, poor ROI
  • PV+Wind (Off-Grid): Better, still wasteful
  • PV+Wind+Gen: Expensive O&M
  • Wind+Gen: Inefficient, spatially demanding
  • PV+Grid: Efficient, space-heavy
  • PV+Wind+Grid: Best compromise

Best Hybrid Model: Output & Economics

  • 250 kW PV, 12 wind turbines, 185 kW converter
  • 768,877 kWh/year generated
  • Grid covers 34.3%
  • 1.4% excess electricity
  • CO₂ reduced to 13,489 kg/year
  • ROI in 5 years | Cost: ~$341,069

Optimization & Smart Features

  • IoT for real-time control and predictive maintenance
  • Load forecasting via smart meters
  • Thin-film insulation to reduce compressor runtime
  • Remote adjustment for demand variation

Conclusion

This model provides a scalable solution to rural food storage challenges with clean energy, ROI within 5 years, and up to 75% energy independence.
