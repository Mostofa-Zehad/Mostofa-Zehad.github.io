Grid-Connected PV Microgrid with Battery Storage

1. Introduction

Amid rising energy demands and increasing pressure to decarbonize power systems, grid-connected photovoltaic (PV) microgrids offer an ideal blend of sustainability, resilience, and economic feasibility. This project focuses on developing and analyzing a PV-based microgrid integrated with battery storage using PSCAD simulation tools. The objective is to supply 24-hour energy by efficiently coordinating solar generation during the day and battery discharge at night.

2. System Design and Architecture

The microgrid consists of a 45 kW solar inverter, 132 SPR-X-21-345 PV modules, and 390 batteries rated at 48V and 10Ah. The system is designed to operate in dual-mode: solar-driven operation during the day and battery-driven supply during nighttime or low irradiation periods.

3. Converter Design and Control

Power electronics are critical for voltage regulation and load matching. The system employs three key converters:

  • Boost Converter: Increases PV voltage for stable DC bus interfacing.
  • Buck-Boost Converter: Charges and discharges the battery as needed.
  • Active Rectifier: Converts DC to high-quality three-phase AC output.

Bode plots confirm system stability by showing positive phase and gain margins across converter loops.

4. Battery Storage Strategy

The battery bank supports 140 kWh of energy delivery over 12 hours during non-solar periods. Charging occurs during solar surplus, and discharging covers evening and early morning demand.

5. Simulation Results and Analysis

PSCAD simulations validate system performance under dynamic loading and irradiance scenarios. The microgrid efficiently balances power among PV, battery, and load through active control.

  • Daytime: PV power meets demand while charging the battery.
  • Nighttime: Battery power discharges to meet the load.
  • Peak Loads: Both PV and battery participate to support higher consumption periods.

6. Economic Assessment

Cost estimation and return on investment analysis confirm that the system is economically practical. The investment is well-justified through substantial annual savings and a short payback horizon:

  • Total System Cost: $118,000
  • Annual Savings: $26,981.32
  • Payback Period: 4.37 years
  • ROI: 22.88%

7. Conclusion

This project demonstrates the technical reliability and economic feasibility of a grid-connected PV microgrid with battery storage. The design ensures continuous energy supply, clean power quality, and cost savings. It is a scalable model ideal for remote areas, campuses, and smart grid pilot zones seeking renewable integration with high reliability.
