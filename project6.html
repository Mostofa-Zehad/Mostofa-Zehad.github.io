Introduction

This project develops a grid-connected photovoltaic (PV) microgrid with battery storage to address clean energy needs and grid resilience. The system design ensures 24-hour operation through intelligent control, solar generation, and energy storage coordination.

System Design and Architecture

Designed with 132 SPR-X-21-345 PV modules (45 kW) and 390 batteries (48V, 10Ah), the microgrid operates in solar and battery modes depending on irradiance. PSCAD modeling helped fine-tune integration of converters and battery banks.

Converter Design and Control

The control system uses three converters for efficient power flow:

  • Boost Converter: Matches PV voltage with DC bus.
  • Buck-Boost Converter: Manages battery charge/discharge.
  • Active Rectifier: Delivers three-phase AC output.

Bode plots verified stable operation for all converter feedback loops.

Battery Storage Strategy

The 140 kWh battery supports night-time loads with smart charging during the day. PSCAD analysis showed reliable SOC performance.

Simulation Results and Analysis

Power flow analysis under varied scenarios showed effective energy balancing:

  • Day: PV supplies load + charges battery
  • Night: Battery powers the load
  • Peaks: Combined supply from PV and battery

Economic Assessment

  • System Cost: $118,000
  • Annual Savings: $26,981.32
  • Payback: 4.37 years
  • ROI: 22.88%

7. Conclusion

This PSCAD-modeled PV-battery microgrid proves efficient, stable, and financially viable for real-world deployment in schools, rural sites, and smart city nodes.
