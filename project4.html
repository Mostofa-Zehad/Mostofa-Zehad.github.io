Hybrid PV and Low-E Glass Retrofit for Sustainable Schools in Bangladesh

Overview

This project evaluates the techno-economic and environmental impact of deploying rooftop photovoltaic (PV) systems and Low-E coated window retrofitting across schools in Bangladesh. Simulations were conducted using HOMER Pro and HelioScope on representative schools to assess performance, payback, and carbon reductions.

1. Problem Statement & Objectives

Public schools in Bangladesh face challenges due to inefficient energy use, high cooling loads, and unreliable grid access. Diesel backup is costly and polluting. This study proposes a hybrid system combining PV electricity generation with thermal comfort improvements from Low-E glass retrofitting.

2. Solar Potential & Energy Profiles

Bangladesh receives ample solar radiation (avg. 4.5–5.5 kWh/m²/day). Monthly clearness index and irradiance patterns suggest high feasibility for rooftop installations across school infrastructures.

Energy profiles show high variance in seasonal demand between secondary and primary schools. Load data was collected from Dhaka schools to define sizing strategies.

3. Environmental Impact

Annual greenhouse gas emissions were modeled for PV-only, diesel-only, and hybrid configurations. Results show up to 75% reduction in emissions for the hybrid system.

4. Low-E Glass Performance

Spectral transmission analysis shows Low-E windows block high infrared heat while allowing visible light. This reduces indoor temperature by 2–3°C without affecting natural daylight.

5. Rooftop PV Layout

HelioScope layout for Udayan School optimizes panel spacing and inverter placement. The simulated array meets up to 50% of daily electricity needs under standard test conditions.

6. Financial Summary

Simulation results indicate a hybrid system ROI of 6.4 years for secondary schools and 7.8 years for primary schools. Low-E retrofits contribute up to 14% cooling cost savings annually.

7. Conclusion & Impact

  • 28% electricity savings from rooftop PV systems
  • Up to 14% cooling energy savings via Low-E glass
  • Up to 18 tons CO₂ saved annually per school
  • ROI within 6.5 years for most secondary institutions
  • Indoor temperatures reduced by 2–3°C in test rooms
