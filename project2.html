EV Charging Infrastructure Performance Evaluation

Overview

This project evaluates the operational performance of 305 EV stations across a span of 41 months. It investigates charging inefficiencies, identifies bottlenecks, analyzes power consumption efficiency, highlights no-power session impacts, and performs resilience modeling using statistical and simulation-based methods. The study offers data-driven insights to optimize station utilization, enhance energy delivery, and guide infrastructure planning.

Scope and Methodology

  • Stations: 305 monitored
  • Sessions: 17,500+
  • Time Period: 41 months
  • Key Metrics: Utilization, power loss, CO₂ savings
  • Techniques: Correlation analysis, regression, Monte Carlo simulation

Performance Highlights

Power Loss

⦿ 27% of stations experienced active session failure.

Idle Time

⦿ 38%+ average idle time in urban zones

Provider Risk

⦿ ChargePoint handled 80% of energy, posing a 19% risk if it fails

Environmental Impact

The optimized EV charging infrastructure reduced CO₂ emissions by up to 120 tons/month based on the U.S. EPA factor of 0.43 kg CO₂/kWh.

⦿ 13,000+ L gasoline saved

⦿ 18 cars emissions offset

⦿ 2000 trees equivalent

⦿ Renewable-powered stations had emissions as low as 0.02 kg CO₂/kWh, while coal-based grids reached 0.7 kg CO₂/kWh.

⦿ Winter data revealed a 15% rise in session duration but a 10% drop in delivered energy, highlighting seasonal performance gaps.

Station Rankings

We identified the top-performing, bottom-performing, and no-power-duration stations in terms of energy efficiency and utilization:

  • Top stations (e.g., CP-192841010698) achieved utilization rates above 0.75 and delivered over 50 kWh per session.
  • Bottom 10% stations contributed less than 5% of network energy output while occupying nearly 20% of the infrastructure.
  • No-power durations exceeded 45 hours at some stations, indicating major idle time and efficiency loss.
  • Winter months showed up to 15% longer charging sessions and 10% lower energy delivery due to seasonal inefficiencies.

Key Recommendations

  • Enable predictive maintenance alerts using ML
  • Diversify charging network operators
  • Incentivize solar-PV integration with fast-charging
  • Adopt dynamic scheduling based on real-time demand
